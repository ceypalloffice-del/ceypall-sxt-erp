# CeyPall + St. Xavier ERP — The Simple User Manual

*Written so anyone can understand it — even a 10-year-old.*

---

## What is this thing?

Think of the ERP as **one big notebook for the whole business**, but on a computer. Instead of writing money, jobs, and stock in different books, everything lives in one place. It lives on the internet at **ceypall-sxt-erp.vercel.app**, so you can open it from any computer or phone.

There are **two companies** inside it:
- **St. Xavier Timber (SXT)** — dries timber in kilns and treats it with chemicals so it doesn't rot.
- **CeyPall (CPL)** — makes wooden pallets (the flat wooden platforms boxes sit on).

---

## Getting in

1. Open the website.
2. Type your **email** and **password**.
3. Click **Sign in**. That's it — you're inside.

If you're new, someone (the director) will set up your account first.

---

## The Company Switcher (very important!)

At the top of every page there's a switcher with three choices:

| Choice | What it shows | Colour |
|---|---|---|
| **St. Xavier** | Only SXT's stuff | Blue |
| **CeyPall** | Only CPL's stuff | Green |
| **Consolidated** | BOTH companies added together | Grey |

Whatever you pick, **every page changes to show only that company**. If numbers look wrong, first check which company you're looking at!

Some menu items only appear for one company:
- **Pallets** and **Materials** → only for CeyPall
- **Treatment Rates, Treatment Quotes, KD Suite, CPI Suite** → only for St. Xavier

## The Pages (left to right in the menu)

### 🏠 Dashboard
The home page. It shows the big picture: how much money customers owe us, what's been sold, what stock is running low. Look here first every morning.

### 👥 Customers
The list of everyone who **buys from us**. Each customer shows how much they still owe us. Click a customer to see their details.

### 🔨 Jobs
A **job** is one piece of work — like "dry 500 pieces of timber for Company X" or "build 200 pallets". Each job has a number (like `JOB-0012`). Jobs collect their costs so we can see if we made money on them.

### 🧾 Invoices
An **invoice** is the bill we send a customer: "You bought this, please pay this much." Here you can:
- See all invoices and whether they're **paid** or **unpaid**.
- Click an invoice number to open it and see every line.
- Click **Print** to get the official **TAX INVOICE** (with VAT) to give to the customer.

### 📦 Products
The list of things we **sell** — timber services, pallet types, and so on, with their prices.

### 🪵 Pallets *(CeyPall only)*
The pallet designs we make — sizes, how much wood each one needs, and what each one costs to build.

### 🧱 Materials *(CeyPall only)*
The wood and parts used to build pallets — planks, blocks, nails — and their prices.

### 💧 Treatment Rates *(St. Xavier only)*
The price list for treating timber. "If a customer brings this much timber, we charge this much."

### 📋 Treatment Quotes *(St. Xavier only)*
When a customer asks "how much would it cost?", we make a **quote** here — a price offer. If they say yes, it becomes a job.

### 🔥 KD Suite *(St. Xavier only)*
KD = **Kiln Drying** (a kiln is a giant oven that dries wood slowly). This section tracks each **batch** of timber going through the kiln — when it went in, when it came out, and the treatment details.

### 🧪 CPI Suite *(St. Xavier only)*
Tools for the chemical treatment business: cost calculators, chemical mixing calculators, price lists, competitor prices, and reports. Use the calculators to work out what a treatment job should cost and what to charge.

### 🚚 Suppliers
The list of everyone we **buy from** — the opposite of customers. Shows how much we still owe them.

### 📝 Purchase Orders
A **PO** is our promise to buy something: "Dear supplier, please send us 100 planks, we'll pay this much." Make a PO here before ordering, so there's a record of what was agreed.

### 💸 Vendor Bills
When a supplier sends **us** a bill, it goes here. This is money we have to pay out.

### 🪙 Petty Cash
The small cash box for little everyday costs — tea, tuk-tuk fares, small repairs. Write every small expense here so no money "disappears".

### 📊 Inventory
Everything sitting in our stores right now — how many of each item, where it is, and what it cost. Stock numbers update automatically when things move in or out. If something is running low, the Dashboard warns you.

### 🏭 Warehouses
The list of places where stock is kept (yards, stores, sheds). Every inventory item lives in one of these.

### ⚗️ Chemicals
The treatment chemicals — how much we have and how much has been used.

## Golden rules

1. **Check the company switcher first.** Half of all "the numbers are wrong!" moments are just looking at the wrong company.
2. **Never invent numbers.** If a page is empty, it's empty because no real data has been entered yet — that's normal for a new system.
3. **Money looks like this:** `LKR 1,172,880` — no cents. A minus in front means money going out or owed.
4. **Everything has a number.** Jobs, invoices, and POs all get their own code (like `INV-0045`). Use these codes when talking to each other — it avoids mix-ups.
5. **The computer does the maths.** Balances, totals, and profits are calculated automatically. You never type a "total" anywhere — you enter the pieces, and the ERP adds them up.

## If something goes wrong

- **Can't log in?** Check your email/password, then ask the director to reset it.
- **Page looks empty?** Check the company switcher; the data might be under the other company.
- **A number looks wrong?** Don't fix it by typing over it — find the invoice/bill/movement that caused it and fix *that*. The totals will correct themselves.
